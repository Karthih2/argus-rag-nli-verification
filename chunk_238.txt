At least on paper, Article 52 of the law also expressly forbade the production of “content that is harmful to the physical or psychological health of minors such as that advocating obscenity, pornography, violence, cults, superstitions, gambling, inducements to suicide, terrorism, separatism or extremism.” Again, this has yet to be tested in court, but it would seem to forbid Chinese animators from even working on such material, and risks shutting down the lucrative “flight film” subcontracting that continues to thrive in many Chinese animation houses, even in the age of the internet. Presumably, the vulnerability of online communications has meant that physical flight film deliveries have continued, although the assets being transported may now be hard drives rather than cels.
