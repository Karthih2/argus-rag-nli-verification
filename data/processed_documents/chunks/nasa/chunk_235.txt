A 2020 Five Year Plan for the Film Industry called for China to become a “strong film nation” (dianying qiangguo), calling for a form of cultural autarky – programming in films and television to be locally made and locally focused, protected from rivals by quotas and bans, creating local blockbuster successes by removing much foreign competition.13 Far from being transnational, the Chinese market began to drift towards a model that might be better described as hyperlocal, resolutely focused on domestic tastes and interests, with little concern for likely appeal overseas.
