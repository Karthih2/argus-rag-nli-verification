In primarily focusing on the lives of young, usually working-class women, Ghibli places an inherent value on their experiences. I argue that the fact that any of these characters received entire dedicated films is enough to suggest feminist intentions of empowerment. Rather than striving to be the most powerful, or to become the most wealthy, these characters seek happiness and contentment in a more low - key way that is drawn from a compassion for their surroundings, whether it comes in the form of saving the forest or enjoying a cup of hot chocolate.
