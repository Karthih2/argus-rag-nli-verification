China was closed to Japanese animation for many years, until Deng Xiaoping’s reforms at the end of the 1970s led to a sudden thaw. The first Japanese animated film to reach China post-thaw was Tatsu no Ko Tarō (1979, Tarō the Dragon Boy), which was screened as part of a Japan Week at a Beijing film festival. It arrived shortly before producers and investors keen to investigate the newly “opened” Chinese market, to see if they could move some of their work to a territory with cheaper labor costs. Mochinaga Tadahito, a Japanese animator with long-standing China contacts and a long history of subcontracting work for the American company Rankin/Bass, was present with the trade delegation, and plainly suffering from split allegiances.
