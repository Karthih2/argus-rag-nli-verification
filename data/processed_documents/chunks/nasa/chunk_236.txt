But Chinese legislation began to push them to be exactly that, aided to some extent by a 2021 consumer report from Jiangsu that identified 1,465 issues arising from a test sample of 21 cartoons.14 Not all were Japanese – one of the offenders was the British children’s series Peppa Pig, already in trouble for featuring on “gangster” tattoos in 2018, while several others were homegrown productions – but the general tone of the report recommended adequate provisions in place to shield children from incidences of dangerous behaviour or imitable violence.
