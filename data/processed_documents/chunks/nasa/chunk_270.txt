The shapes of animation are dynamic and undefined by nature – automatically defamiliarized by their own instability, even when not in motion. Though defamiliarization is helpful and relevant to this article as a standalone method of filmic analysis, I will be utilising it in conjunction with a method from another framework in order to effectively study why domestic moments in Ghibli films are both freshly presented and sensually appealing.
