In hearing the sound of her pressing the flowers, viewers understand how they would feel to the touch, and are further immersed in the sensual elements of the process. The use of similes in the script – ‘like liquid gold’ – also seems to encourage phenomenological thought, allowing the viewer to also make subjective connection s between the safflowers and what they have personally experienced. Defamiliarisation, much like in the pineapple scene, is brought forth through closeups and long takes, in this case of the safflowers themselves.
