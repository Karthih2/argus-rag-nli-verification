As an art for m reliant entirely on pictorial representation in motion, I argue that two -dimensional animation is a key medium in terms of its ability to defamiliarize. By abstracting a familiar image into a collection of colours and lines, the viewer is unconsciously f orced to analyse the frame and determine what exactly is being depicted. This then challenges them to newly recognise an image that has previously been second nature, in a way that direct photographic representation may not. This is exemplified in Eisenstein’s notion of ‘plasticity’, defined by Pasi Valiaho as the ability of animated forms to ‘at any moment [be] susceptible to stretching, bending and rebounding, if not to total shape-shifting’ 27.
