This museum is of a modest size, attracting only 650,000 visitors a year 45 in comparison to Disneyworld’s 52 million annual attendees 46, further highlighting Ghibli’s connection with the small scale and personal. This also stretches to the content inside of the museum; rather than traditionally spectacular rides, short films and exhibitions make up most of the attractions. Unsurprisingly, the powerful legacy of Studio Ghibli looms large over anime films from other studios that have been released in recent years, resulting in homage via aesthetics and subject matter, including portraying the mundane as spectacular.
