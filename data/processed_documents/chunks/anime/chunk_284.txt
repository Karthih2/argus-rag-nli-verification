Narratively, Only Yesterday presents many of the conventionally feminine experiences of Taeko (periods and arguing with her sisters over new clothes). To be clear, this is not to imply that they are inactive in their own narratives because of their femininity; I argue that one of Ghibli’s more recognisable features is its treatment of the feminine as powerful, particularly that of working - class women. Understandably, the feminist readings mentioned tend towards looking at the heroism of the female characters when removed from a domestic context – for instance, when in battle or plunged into fantastical worlds. However, this context is nonetheless frequently depicted in numerous Ghibli films
