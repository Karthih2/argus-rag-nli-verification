Journal of Anime and Manga Studies Volume 6, Issue 1 34 In spite of such forebodings, the period 2015-2020 saw a brief flurry of Chinese co-productions and even direct Chinese investment in Japanese animation. With internet access reaching 50% of the Chinese population in 2015, content providers keen to capitalise on the popularity of Japanese animation were prepared to fund Japanese companies to make or at least contribute to animation for the Chinese market. Such investments, such as Tencent’s payment to a Japanese company to produce Bloodivores (2016) were themselves compromised by continued changes and refinements to Chinese law.
