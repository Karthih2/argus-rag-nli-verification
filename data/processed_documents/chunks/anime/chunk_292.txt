As the camera pans up to Howl’s face, we see that his hair is long and light, and that he is wearing earrings and a necklace. This combination of gender signifiers suggests that Howl is not a slave to performing masculinity – while male, the way he appears removes the threat of masculine violence. The fact that he ‘saves’ Sophie in this scene could be interpreted as upholding stereotypical gender roles.
