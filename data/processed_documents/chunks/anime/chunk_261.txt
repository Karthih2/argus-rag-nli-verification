This allows for a clean aesthetic reminiscent of the simplistic ‘emakimono’ style of drawing in Japan which ‘freed’ artists from ‘over illustrative details’ in depicting people 21. This was markedly different from the complex use of CGI and 3D animation films by Pixar emerging at the same time - Toy Story 2 22 the same year, for instance. The plot of the film features few fantasy elements, instead fixating on the goings on of an average Japanese family. As somewhat of a visual outlier in the Ghibli filmography, I believe it both aesthetically and thematically intensifies and abstracts the notion of domesticity in the Studio’s films to its purest form.
