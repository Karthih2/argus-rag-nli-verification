Though this truly comes to fruition in their anthology of short films Modest Heroes (2018, Chīsana Eiyū: Kani to Tamago to Tōmei Ningen) 49, their first feature Mary and the Witch’s Flower (2017, Meari to Majo no Hana)50 foregrounds this with its focus on accessible nature, particularly the sensory aspects of gardening (much like Only Yesterday). As well as featuring a young female protagonist, a trope common to Ghibli works, the film is rooted in its pastoral setting, its title coming from the small blue flower that the protagonist Mary comes across while exploring the woods near her grandmother’s house.
