Journal of Anime and Manga Studies Volume 2 2 Introduction: To begin, I would like to clarify my usage of the words ‘spectacular’ and ‘mundane’, upon which this article depends. Rather than implications of boredom implied by some definitions, I am using the term ‘mundane’ to mean ‘characterised by the practical, transitory, and ordinary’ 1 – domestic chores and other everyday occurrences, for example. Similarly, while spectacle is often associated with excitement, I intend to use the definition of the term that I believe relates most closely to its use in film scholarship: ‘something exhibi ted to view as unusual, notable, or entertaining’ 2.
