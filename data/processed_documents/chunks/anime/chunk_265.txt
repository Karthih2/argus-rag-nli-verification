As well as layering the gazes of the characters for comedic effect, the scene also uses the idea of spectatorship to convey the main thesis of the film. Through this sequence, My Neighbors the Yamadas posits that the everyday goings on of a family can often be more exciting and compelling than what is typically constructed as spectacle, e.g. a sports game or mainstream movie.
