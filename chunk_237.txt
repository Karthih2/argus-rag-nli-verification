The revised Law on the Protection of Minors (2020)15 emphasised that a minor in China was anyone under the age of eighteen, effectively wiping out much of the risqué content for late teens that constitutes half of all anime output. Although not yet tested in court, the law also effectively criminalised parents who allowed their children (up to the age of 18) from even watching unsuitable content. The law shut down access to such material at both ends, outlawing its transmission, but also forbidding under-18s from even owning streaming accounts.
