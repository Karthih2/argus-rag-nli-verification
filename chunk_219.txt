Journal of Anime and Manga Studies Volume 6, Issue 1 Many discussions of the transnational make the supposition that we are approaching some sort of teleological perfection – that the more transnational a subject becomes, the better it is. To which I would ask: better for whom? Some pundits speak of the happy ending for the Chinese animation industry, with its massive domestic sales figures and lucrative franchises. However, I am going to tell you what many in the Japanese animation industry would regard as a tale of woe, as a territory that anime companies regarded as a low-cost outsourcing service first became a possible export client, and then an investor and strange attractor, and ultimately a closed market that tried to exclude as much Japanese animation as possible.
