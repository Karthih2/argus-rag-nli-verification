Here, the Ghibli preoccupation with everyday activities reached its peak, as domestic Japanese customs and familial relationships are given room to exist only for themselves, rather than to forward a more eventful plot. Rather than a linear cause and effect narrative, Takahata utilises a more episodic structure. Each short segment has a grandiose, tongue in cheek title such as ‘A Family Torn Apart’ or ‘Patriarchal Supremacy Restored’ – these humorously contrast with the mundane content of the shorts.
