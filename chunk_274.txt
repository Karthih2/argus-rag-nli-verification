This is highlighted effectively in the Japanese title of the film, Romanised as ‘Omoide Poro Poro’, which translates to ‘Memories Come Tumbling Down’. In this sense, Only Yesterday fits Gilles Deleuze’s notion of the ‘time image’32, in which a film focuses on identification with the protagonist’s subjective experience of time – a mode that further emphasises the importance of the personal, and therefore often the ordinary.
